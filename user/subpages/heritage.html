Explore the Heritage of Odisha


Heritage Sites of Odisha

Konark Sun Temple
Konark
UNESCO World Heritage site, built in the 13th century. Famous for its massive chariot-shaped architecture with 24 wheels and intricate stone carvings.
Dhauli Shanti Stupa
Bhubaneswar
Peace pagoda built atop Dhauli hills, marking the site of ancient Kalinga War. Features Ashokan rock edicts from the 3rd century BCE.
Ratnagiri Buddhist Excavation
Jajpur
Buddhist ruins from the 6th-9th century. Features stupas, monasteries, and sculptures that provide insight into ancient Buddhist culture.
Chilika Lake
Khurda
Asia's largest brackish water lagoon, home to thousands of migratory birds. Known for its rich biodiversity.
Lingaraj Temple
Bhubaneswar
One of the oldest and largest temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, a stunning example of Kalinga architecture.
Mukteswar Temple
Bhubaneswar
Known as the 'Gem of Odisha temples', it is famous for its architectural beauty, intricate carvings, and sculptural work.
Bhubaneswar Caves
Bhubaneswar
Ancient rock-cut caves with fascinating carvings, some of which are Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain in origin.
Baleshwar Temple
Balasore
An ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, featuring a unique architectural design and carvings.
Similipal Tiger Reserve
Mayurbhanj
A pristine wildlife sanctuary and tiger reserve, home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including Royal Bengal tigers.
